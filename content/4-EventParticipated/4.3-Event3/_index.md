# AWS EDUCATIONAL VISIT: CLOUD ARCHITECTURE AND CAREER TRENDS – JULY 4, 2026

## Purpose of the Event

The educational visit to the AWS Vietnam Office was organized to provide students with an opportunity to learn directly about the professional environment of the cloud-computing industry.

Through presentations delivered by experienced professionals, participants gained practical insights into cloud architecture, Artificial Intelligence, career development, and the skills required for future technology professionals.

In addition to technical knowledge, the event introduced participants to the AWS working culture, real-world problem-solving approaches, and the importance of continuous learning in the technology industry.

## Speakers

The event featured the following speakers and industry professionals:

- **Nguyen Gia Hung** – Head of Solutions Architecture at AWS Vietnam and founder of the First Cloud AI Journey program.
- **Banh Cam Vinh** – Data Engineer at Renova Cloud and AWS Community Builder.
- **Nhu Tan** – Account Manager at Amazon Web Services Vietnam, specializing in startup customers.
- **Khang Nguyen** – Solutions Architect at Cloud Kinetics and an alumnus of Swinburne Vietnam.
- **Nguyen Tran Minh Duy** – Industry Liaison Officer at Swinburne Vietnam.

## Key Highlights

### The Development of Cloud Computing

The speakers discussed the development of cloud computing and its role in digital transformation.

The key points included:

- Cloud Computing is an important part of digital transformation.
- Cloud services help organizations deploy systems more quickly and flexibly.
- AWS provides services for computing, storage, databases, networking, security, and Artificial Intelligence.
- Technology decisions should be based on system requirements rather than current trends.

This session helped me understand that Cloud Computing is not simply about moving servers to the Internet. It is also about building scalable, secure, reliable, and cost-effective systems.

### Academic Knowledge and Real-World Practice

An important topic was the difference between academic exercises and real business environments.

In practical projects:

- Data may be incomplete or inconsistent.
- Customer requirements may change frequently.
- Systems must support scalability and high availability.
- Technical decisions must balance security, performance, and cost.
- Engineers must understand the business problem before selecting a technical solution.

This helped me realize that understanding the nature of a problem is more valuable than knowing how to operate a particular tool.

### AI and Future Career Trends

The speakers also discussed the rapid development of Artificial Intelligence and its influence on technology careers.

The main ideas included:

- AI is becoming an important assistant in software development.
- AI tools can support research, coding, documentation, and data analysis.
- AI can improve productivity but cannot completely replace human judgment.
- Users must validate AI-generated results.
- Strong foundational knowledge is still required to use AI effectively.

I learned that AI should be used as a supporting tool. Engineers must continue to apply critical thinking, technical knowledge, and professional responsibility.

### Networking and Career Development

The event emphasized the importance of professional networking.

Career opportunities depend not only on technical knowledge but also on:

- Communication and teamwork.
- Participation in technology communities.
- Connections with industry professionals.
- Workshops and practical projects.
- A professional personal profile.
- Willingness to learn and share knowledge.

Professional relationships can create more opportunities for learning, internships, and future employment.

## What I Learned

### Personal Development

The event showed that career development requires a combination of:

- Technical knowledge.
- Adaptability.
- Discipline.
- Communication skills.
- Continuous learning.
- Initiative and responsibility.

Success does not depend only on the number of technologies a person knows. It also depends on the ability to apply knowledge to real-world problems.

### Problem-Solving Mindset

One of the most important lessons was to analyze the problem before selecting a technology.

A suitable problem-solving process can include:

- Identifying user and business requirements.
- Reviewing the current data and system.
- Determining security, performance, and cost requirements.
- Selecting an appropriate architecture.
- Testing the solution before deployment.
- Monitoring and optimizing the system after release.

This approach helps prevent technology choices based only on popularity or trends.

### Soft Skills

Technical professionals also need important soft skills:

- Clear communication with colleagues and customers.
- The ability to explain technical ideas.
- Teamwork in project environments.
- Time and task management.
- Personal branding and professional networking.

### Lifelong Learning

The technology industry changes continuously, so learning does not end after graduation.

Cloud professionals should:

- Follow new technologies.
- Practice regularly.
- Read official documentation.
- Attend courses and workshops.
- Build personal projects.
- Share knowledge with the community.

## Applying the Knowledge

After the educational visit, I identified several actions that I could apply to my learning and career development:

- Build a structured AWS learning roadmap.
- Complete hands-on labs to strengthen technical knowledge.
- Understand the purpose of each AWS service instead of only memorizing procedures.
- Use AI as a tool for research and documentation.
- Validate AI-generated information and code.
- Participate in AWS events and communities.
- Improve communication, presentation, and teamwork skills.
- Continue preparing for AWS certifications.
- Build more practical Cloud Computing projects.
